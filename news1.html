The rising number of pigeons at Kelburn Campus overtakes the amount of students attending class


KELBURN - The rising number of pigeons on the Kelburn Victoria University of Wellington Campus is said to have overtaken the number of University Students that are attending classes. A professor working at the University stated that the number of pigeons on the campus may be affecting the students class attendance. This could be a result of the pigeons taking up space in the already small main courtyard. Another possible reason could be the fight for free left-over food, as this could have the students and pigeons at quarrel.

Victoria University of Wellington Student Brooklyn, 19, shared his traumatic encounter with the pigeons on Kelburn Campus. "It was on a Thursday, I was just chilling outside the Hub in the courtyard enjoying my Maki Mono sushi because half price food always tastes better. Suddenly a herd of pigeons swooped in and took my teriyaki chicken. I tried to fight them off as I had been to the Kelburn Rec Centre that morning but it was no use. They ended up taking off with over half of my pack. That same day is when I started up the club 'Pigeon H8ters' so that like minded students could rally together. In the several months that the club has been running we have formed a group of three people so I think the success of this group will continue to grow as we fight for student rights."
